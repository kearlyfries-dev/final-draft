Why I Hike

There is something irreplaceable about earning a view. No elevator, no shortcut, just you, a trail, and whatever you can carry on your back. Hiking grounds me when life feels overwhelming and reminds me how vast the world really is.

Growing up in Utah I was surrounded by some of the most spectacular terrain on earth. Zion, Bryce Canyon, the Uintas, these places shaped who I am. The outdoors became my second classroom.

The Hiker's Gear Guide

Good gear makes the difference between a miserable slog and a great adventure. Here is a breakdown of what goes in my pack:

  1. The Ten Essentials
    • Navigation such as a map, compass, or GPS
    • Sun protection including sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat
    • Insulation and extra layers
    • Illumination with a headlamp and spare batteries
    • First aid kit
    • Fire starter
    • Repair tools and a knife
    • Extra food and nutrition
    • Extra water and a filter
    • Emergency shelter such as a bivy or space blanket
  2. Footwear Considerations
    • Trail runners are lightweight and great for well maintained paths
    • Mid cut boots offer ankle support for rocky terrain
    • Waterproof boots are a must for shoulder season snow
    • Gaiters keep debris out on dusty or snowy trails
  3. Clothing System
    • Base layer: moisture wicking synthetic or merino wool
    • Mid layer: fleece or down jacket
    • Outer layer: wind and rain shell
    • Convertible pants let you hike in shorts and zip on legs when it cools
  4. Tech and Extras
    • Trekking poles save your knees on descents
    • Satellite communicator such as a Garmin inReach
    • Portable battery pack
    • A camera or a phone with a great lens

Favorite Utah Trails

Angels Landing

Zion National Park  ·  5.4 miles  ·  Strenuous

A chain assisted final push leads to a breathtaking perch high above Zion Canyon. The exposure at the top is unlike anything else in Utah and the views make every step worth it. A permit is required for the final section.

The Narrows

Zion National Park  ·  Up to 16 miles  ·  Moderate

You wade through the Virgin River as towering 1,000 foot slot canyon walls close in on both sides. There is no trail here because the river is the trail. One of the most unique hiking experiences in the world.

Mt. Timpanogos

Uinta Wasatch Cache National Forest  ·  14.8 miles  ·  Strenuous

Utah's most beloved alpine summit. The route passes a glacial cirque lake and ends on a dramatic ridgeline with panoramic views stretching across the entire Wasatch Front. A true classic for any Utah hiker.
